Metam Mobile App Case Study

We had five team members who worked together to create Metam.

We started out planning to develop an app that helps people prioritize their responsibilities and tasks. We created a Proto-persona for a father of 3 who was struggling with his work-life balance.

Several of us felt our initial problem had underlying emotional solutions and we wanted to create a problem that could be solved easily with software.

We followed the Design process throughout, and iterated when we needed to make some updates and reevaluate.

User Insight and Problem Statement

While our persona remained mainly the same throughout our User Persona and User Journey process, our focus of the problem and a solution shifted through our Ideation phase based upon User Research and Competitive Analysis, and our desire to create a solution solved with software.

Research Results Our research showed 71% of those surveyed said they needed help with improving work/life balance and 91% said they need to prioritize tasks. I conducted several user interviews from the Research Plan we collaboraated in creating. Zoom was used to conduct them. Since they were virtual I needed to rely on tone of voice and only on facial expressions to get emotional clues from the interviewees. Fortunatly recording the interviews allowed further review if needed.
Competitive Analysis While our initial research revealed there was more of demand for a task mgmt app, our competitive analysis revealed that this market was simply too was too crowded. So we targeted the motivational app market which you see here on the left side of the image. And Indirect competitors on the right side.
